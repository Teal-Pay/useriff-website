Privacy Policy

This Privacy Notice describes how Teal Pay Global, Inc. and our affiliates (collectively, “Gemm,” “we,” and “us”) collect, use, disclose, transfer, store, retain or otherwise process your information when you, whether as an individual sole proprietor or on behalf of a business entity, apply or sign up for a Gemm user account and other services through Gemm’s website or applications (collectively, the “Services”).

Please review our GLBA Privacy Notice for Financial Customers to understand how we share your data.

If you are a Gemm user, this privacy notice applies to you. It explains what data we collect about you, what we may do with it, when and why we share it with others, how long we keep it, how we secure it, what cookies are and what they and similar technologies do, what data is collected about you by third-party service providers, and what choices you have to control your data.

By using our Services, you consent to these data practices. If you use our Services on behalf of your company, that company consents too.

This Privacy Notice applies to information collected in connection with your access to and use of our Services. Please read this Privacy Notice carefully. By continuing to interact with our Services, you are consenting to the practices described in this Privacy Notice.

INFORMATION WE COLLECT ABOUT YOU

We use your data to make Gemm’s products and services work better for you and for others. This describes what data we get about you, which can vary depending on where you live.

Information You Provide

We use data you put into our systems when you open and use an account or other systems, like financial or tax information, information that identifies who you are, transaction information, and any other data you give us.

We may collect information you provide when you:

  • Make a request to receive information about Gemm;
  • Apply or sign up for a Gemm account or other Services;
  • Go through our identity or account verification process, authenticate into your account, communicate with us, answer our surveys, upload content, or otherwise interact with the Services.

We call this information User Account Data.

Specifically, we collect the following categories of information:

  • Financial information, such as bank account information and payment card numbers;
  • Identification Information, such as name, email address, postal address, signature, phone number, passport number, driver’s license number, Social Security number, Taxpayer Identification number, or other government-issued identification number;
  • Tax information, such as withholding allowances and tax filing status;
  • Transaction Information, such as information about when and where payment transactions occur, the names of the transacting parties, a description of the transactions, the payment or transfer amounts, billing and shipping information, and the devices and payment methods used to complete the transactions;
  • Other Information You Provide, such as information you voluntarily provide to us, including survey responses, participation in contests, promotions, prospective seller marketing forms, suggestions for improvements, referrals, or other actions you perform on the Services.

Information We Collect From Your Use of the Services

We also get data from the devices you use when you interact with our systems, like your location or information about the device you are using. We need this data to help protect your account from hackers, combat fraud, verify that payments are being made or received legally and by you, and improve our products and services.

Data we collect may include:

  • Commercial information, such as information about your payment transactions;
  • Electronic information, such as web browser and device characteristics;
  • Geolocation data, which includes the location of your device;
  • Internet or other electronic network activity information, including how you use and interact with our Services, access time, log-in and log-out information, browser type and language, domain name of your internet service provider, pages visited, content viewed, features used, date and time of use, search terms, and referral website information;
  • Online identifiers, including login information, IP address, device identifier, cookies, beacons, pixel tags, mobile ad identifiers, customer number, unique alias, and similar identifiers;
  • Professional information, such as information you provide about your business; and
  • Inferences drawn from the information above to create a profile about you reflecting, for example, your preferences, characteristics, and behavior.

Information We Collect From Other Sources

We may collect data about you from companies that help us verify your identity, perform credit checks, prevent fraud, or assess risk.

  • Background check information, including credit report information, identity verification information, and information about persons or corporations with whom you have had, currently have, or may have a financial relationship;
  • Credit, compliance and fraud information received from third parties for credit investigation, credit eligibility, identity or account verification, fraud detection, or collection procedures, or as otherwise required by applicable law.

Digital cookies and similar technologies help us improve our Services by recognizing when you have signed in, analyzing how you use our Services, personalizing your experience, and making our advertising more effective.

When you interact with our online services, or open emails we send you, we obtain certain information using automated technologies such as cookies, web server logs, web beacons, and other technologies.

These technologies help us to:

  • Track and understand how you use and interact with our online services and emails;
  • Tailor our online services to your preferences;
  • Measure how useful and effective our services and communications are to you; and
  • Otherwise manage and enhance our products and services.

Your browser can alert you when cookies are placed on your device and how you can stop or disable them. You can learn more about cookies and manage your preferences at allaboutcookies.org.

You can visit the Digital Advertising Alliance WebChoices tool to make those choices.

Some of these providers may also be members of the Network Advertising Initiative, which offers its own opt-out tool for interest-based advertising delivered by member companies.

Third-Party Online Behavioral or Interest-Based Advertising Services Within Mobile Applications
